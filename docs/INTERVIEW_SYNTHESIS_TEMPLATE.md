# Interview Synthesis Template

Status: Template
Last updated: 2026-04-21
Purpose: Turn the first round of 2 student interviews + 1 coach interview into a one-page synthesis that can directly update the research brief.

## How to Use

- Keep this to roughly one page.
- Write only the strongest recurring findings, not every note.
- Prefer concrete episodes and direct quotes over general impressions.
- If the evidence points away from the current claim, say so directly.

## One-Page Synthesis

### 1. Who We Talked To

- **Student 1:** [experience level, practice style, relevant context]
- **Student 2:** [experience level, practice style, relevant context]
- **Coach 1:** [coaching context, student population, relevant context]

### 2. What They Do Today

- Students currently rely on: [mirror / teammate / self-video / delayed coach questions / other]
- Coaches currently observe that students compensate by: [short phrase]

### 3. Most Common Workaround

- **Most common workaround:** [one workaround]
- **Why students use it:** [one sentence]
- **Why it falls short:** [one sentence]

### 4. Most Common Failure

- **Repeated failure mode:** [e.g., students repeat mistakes without noticing in the moment]
- **Concrete example:** [one specific episode]
- **Cost of failure:** [wasted time / bad habit / confusion / confidence loss]

### 5. Adoption Conditions

- Students said they would use a system like this if: [1-2 conditions]
- Students said they would stop using it if: [1-2 conditions]
- Coaches would recommend it if: [1-2 conditions]

### 6. Most Important Quotes

- Student quote 1: "[direct quote]"
- Student quote 2: "[direct quote]"
- Coach quote: "[direct quote]"

### 7. What This Means for Our Problem Framing

- **Primary user:** [solo-practice student / coach / mixed]
- **Primary problem:** [one sentence]
- **Most realistic system role:** [solo-practice aid / coach assistant / review tool]
- **What changed from the old framing:** [one sentence]

### 8. What This Changes in the Brief

- Keep:
  - [claim that is now better supported]
  - [claim that is now better supported]
- Revise:
  - [claim that needs narrowing]
  - [claim that needs stronger wording or evidence]
- Remove:
  - [claim not supported by interviews]

### 9. Decision

- **Does the current positioning still hold?** [yes / no / partially]
- **If yes, why?** [one sentence]
- **If no or partially, what should be reframed?** [one sentence]
